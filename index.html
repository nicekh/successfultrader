1. Open Interest (OI) Analysis

High OI at a strike price: Indicates strong support (PUT side) or resistance (CALL side).
Rising OI with price increase: Indicates strong trend continuation.
Falling OI with price movement: Suggests unwinding and possible reversal.

2. PCR (Put-Call Ratio)

PCR > 1: More put writing, bullish sentiment.
PCR < 1: More call writing, bearish sentiment.
Extreme PCR values: Indicate overbought or oversold conditions.

3. IV (Implied Volatility) & IV Crush

High IV: Option premiums are expensive (good for sellers).
Low IV: Option premiums are cheap (good for buyers).
IV Crush: Happens after major events (earnings, RBI policy), leading to premium decay.

4. Strike Price Selection

For Buying Options: Choose ATM or slightly ITM with high liquidity and rising OI.
For Selling Options: Choose OTM strikes with high OI and strong resistance/support.

5. Institutional Activity

Look at OI changes: Big players often write options at key levels.
Sudden OI spike: Can indicate fresh positions being built.

6. Max Pain Theory

Max Pain is the strike price where most options will expire worthless.
Close price near max pain on expiry day: Good for sellers as premiums decay.

7. Volume Analysis

High volume + high OI: Strong conviction at that strike.
High volume + low OI: Intraday traders may be active, not strong positional play.

8. Hedge Positioning

Check nearby strikes for heavy OI: Institutions hedge positions at key levels.
Sudden shift in OI: Can indicate potential breakouts or breakdowns.
